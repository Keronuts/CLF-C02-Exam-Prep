In this first lesson, letâ€™s explore the AWS Shared Responsibility Model. Security and compliance are shared responsibilities between AWS and their cloud customers.

This shared approach can help lessen the customer's operational weight. AWS operates, manages and optimizes the components from the host operating system and virtualization layer all the way down to the physical security of the availability zone data centers in which the services operate. By default and an infrastructure as a service model, The customer assumes responsibility and management of the guest operating system, including updates, upgrades, fixes and security patches and other supplementary application software, along with the configuration of the cloud firewall solutions provided by AWS. Realize some of the responsibility can be handed back to AWS by using platform or software as a service offerings.

Customers should carefully evaluate the selected services. Their responsibilities vary contingent on the services used. The integration of those services into their IT environments and any relevant laws and regulations. This is highly applicable to the platform as a service PaaS models. The nature of this shared responsibility also provides the flexibility and customer control that permits the deployment at AWS.

To put it simply, AWS's responsibility is security of the cloud, where the customer has responsibility of security in the cloud.

Some shared controls would be Patch management. AWS must Patch and fix configuration flaws within their infrastructure. But customers are often responsible for patching their guest operating systems and applications, primarily in the infrastructure as a service model.

Configuration management preserves the configuration of its data center infrastructure devices, but customers may be responsible for configuring their own guest operating systems, databases and applications.

As far as awareness and training goes, AWS trains AWS employees, But the customer must provide their own training for their own employees. Keep in mind however, AWS has plenty of resources. and certifications to help customers train their employees.

[Video description begins] Topic title: AWS Governance Concepts. Presented by: Michael Shannon. [Video description ends] Governance Defined: Governance can be described as an initiative by which entities are directed and controlled.

Governance is focused on the structure and processes for decision making, accountability, control and activities at the top of the entity. The commercial enterprise or the organization.

Governance influences how an organization's objectives are established and realized, how risk is monitored and assessed, and how performance is optimized.

Governance is a system and process, not a single activity. and therefore successful implementation of a good governance strategy requires a systematic approach that involves strategic planning, risk management and performance management. Governance is a core component of the unique characteristics of a successful organization. For example, their culture.

AWS governance is the capacity to introduce executive board policies and decisions that your cloud environment must observe. AWS governance policy includes the rules for the cloud environment, the definition of risks and alignment with internal organizational policies. A percentage of the governance policies is embedded in all other initiatives across all environments to certify adherence to policy requirements.

Governance of the corporate environment is critical to understand why and how cloud services are consumed. The cloud environment must align with the organization's strategy on cloud service provider utilization. All organizations, regardless of the size or the industry they're in or business sector, need to establish a capability to effectively use cloud services, define policies and standards, understand and mitigate risks, and to approve essential legal, commercial and regulatory requirements. That is governance in a nutshell at Amazon Web Services.
